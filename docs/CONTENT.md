# Content Guidelines & Article Format

---

## Content Philosophy

**Authority over brevity.** Each article explores a topic with depth and actionability.

- **Length:** 800-1500 words (comprehensive, not skimmable)
- **Purpose:** Actionable guidance for founders and decision-makers
- **Tone:** Expert, accessible, conversational (not academic)
- **Search focus:** Target intent words and long-tail queries

### Body Structure

#### 1. Introduction (100-150 words)
- Hook: Why this topic matters for founders
- Problem statement: What challenge this addresses
- Promise: What the article delivers
- Audience: Who should read this

Example:
```markdown
## ¿Qué es ANDE y por qué importa para tu startup?

Lanzar una startup en Uruguay sin conocer ANDE es dejar dinero sobre la mesa.

La Asociación Nacional de Emprendedores (ANDE) es la puerta de entrada al ecosistema 
de financiamiento, mentoría, y redes de inversores. En este artículo, aprenderás cómo 
ANDE puede acelerar tu startup desde día uno.

**Este artículo es para:** Fundadores en etapas tempranas, emprendedores buscando 
capital semilla, y líderes de equipos considerando Uruguay como mercado de entrada.
```

#### 2. Context Section (200-300 words)
- What is the topic? (definition + context)
- Why does it exist?
- What problem does it solve?
- Historical/current state

Example heading: "Entender ANDE: Quién es y cómo nació"

#### 3. Key Points / Deep Dive (400-600 words)
- **2-4 subsections** covering main aspects
- Each subsection: 100-150 words
- Include **specific numbers, dates, programs, deadlines**
- Link to other relevant articles when applicable

Examples:
- "Programas de Financiamiento ANDE"
- "Mentoría y Capacitación"
- "Red de Inversores y Conectividad"

#### 4. Practical Takeaways (150-200 words)
- **Actionable next steps** for the reader
- Who to contact / where to go
- Timeline (if applicable)
- Common mistakes to avoid

Example:
```markdown
## Próximos Pasos: Cómo Acceder a ANDE

1. **Visita ande.com.uy** y revisa los programas activos
2. **Prepara tu pitch de 2 minutos** (problema, solución, equipo, ask)
3. **Aplica a un programa** que encaje con tu etapa actual
4. **Asiste a un Demo Day** (mensual) para exponerte a inversores
5. **Conecta con mentores** del programa que asignación
```

#### 5. Related Resources (50-100 words)
- Links to other articles
- External resources (ANDE site, programs)
- Tools or templates

Example:
```markdown
## Recursos Adicionales

- **Artículo relacionado:** [Capital Semilla: Fuentes y Proceso en Uruguay](/articles/financiamiento/capital-semilla/)
- **Directorio ANDE:** ande.com.uy
- **Demo Days:** calendario en web de ANDE
```

## Metadata Guidelines

### Title Tag (SEO)
Format: `[Topic] — Startups TIC Uruguay | Antonio Galgano Votta`
- Keep under 60 characters
- Lead with main keyword
- Include location (Uruguay) or context (Startups)
- Brand always last

Examples:
- ✅ "ANDE Financiamiento — Startups TIC Uruguay | Antonio Galgano Votta"
- ❌ "ANDE" (too short, no context)
- ❌ "Everything You Need to Know About ANDE in Uruguay" (too long)

### Meta Description
Format: 150-160 characters
- Answer: What does the reader learn?
- Include 1-2 key benefits
- Natural language (not keyword-stuffed)
- Action-oriented

Example:
```
"Guía completa: cómo ANDE acelera tu startup en Uruguay con capital semilla, mentoría, 
y red de inversores. Pasos para acceder y programas actuales."
```

### Page Title (H1)
- Match the topic exactly
- Conversational (not title case formality)
- Actionable if possible

Examples:
- ✅ "¿Qué es ANDE y cómo acceder al financiamiento para tu startup?"
- ✅ "ANNI: Impulsando la innovación en startups de Uruguay"
- ❌ "ANDE" (too bare)

## Writing Style Guide

### Tone & Voice
- **Expert but accessible:** Know your subject; explain without jargon
- **Conversational:** Address reader as "tú" (informal), not "usted" (formal)
- **Action-oriented:** Use imperative when appropriate ("Aplica a...", "Prepara tu...")
- **Transparent:** Acknowledge trade-offs and limitations

### Language
- **Spanish (es-UY)** — Use Uruguay Spanish conventions
- **Active voice** — "ANDE ofrece..." not "Se ofrece..."
- **Specific numbers** — "Hasta $50,000 USD" not "una cantidad significativa"
- **Present tense** — "ANDE es..." not "ANDE será..."

### Structure
- **Headings:** Use H2 (##) for major sections, H3 (###) for subsections
- **Bulleted lists:** Max 5 items per list
- **Bold:** Highlight key terms and concepts first mention
- **Links:** To other articles, external resources, always descriptive text
- **No images** (unless design/layout updated)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Vague language ("significativo", "importante") — be specific
- ❌ Too much theory — prioritize practical takeaways
- ❌ Out-of-date information — verify dates, programs, contacts
- ❌ Broken links — test all URLs before publishing
- ❌ Over-promotion of services — focus on education, not sales

## SEO Optimization Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] **Keyword research:** Main keyword in title, H1, meta description, first 100 words
- [ ] **Internal links:** 2-3 links to related articles
- [ ] **External links:** 2-3 authoritative sources (ANDE, ANNI, gov sites)
- [ ] **Image alt text:** (if images added) Descriptive, keyword-relevant
- [ ] **Headings hierarchy:** H1 → H2 → H3 (no skips)
- [ ] **Meta description:** 150-160 chars, includes keyword and CTA
- [ ] **Readability:** Flesch score 50-60 (conversational, not simplistic)
- [ ] **Mobile:** Test on phone/tablet (responsive design assumed)
- [ ] **Links:** No 404s; external links open in new tab

## Publishing Workflow

1. **Draft article** in `src/articles/[category]/[slug].md`
2. **Build locally:** `npm run build` and verify at localhost:8080
3. **Check styling:** Headings, lists, links render correctly
4. **Test URLs:** Verify /articles/[category]/[slug]/ structure
5. **Verify SEO:** Title, description, canonical, H1 all correct
6. **Spell check:** Spanish grammar & Uruguay conventions
7. **Link verification:** All internal and external links work
8. **Commit with message:** `content: add [article title]`
9. **Push & deploy:** Merge to main → deploy to production

---

## Maintenance

- **Monthly:** Review external links for 404s
- **Quarterly:** Update article with latest program dates, amounts
- **Annually:** Audit for outdated information, refresh top performers
- **Ongoing:** Monitor analytics; update underperforming articles or retire
